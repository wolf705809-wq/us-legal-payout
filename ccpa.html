Data Sovereignty (CCPA)

Notice of Right to Opt-Out of Sale or Sharing of Personal Information
CCPA/CPRA Compliance Disclosure

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), California residents have the right to opt-out of the "sale" or "sharing" of their personal information to third parties. Nodal prioritizes Data Sovereignty and provides this transparent mechanism to exercise your rights.

1. Right to Opt-Out

Nodal synchronizes high-fidelity incident metadata with a curated network of legal service providers to facilitate statutory recovery. While we do not sell data in the traditional sense, this exchange may be classified as "sharing" or "selling" under California law. You have the absolute right to direct us not to sell or share your information at any time.

2. Information We Share

In the preceding 12 months, Nodal has shared the following categories of data for "Statutory Lead Matching" purposes:

  • Identifiers (Name, Contact Information)
  • Commercial Incident Metadata
  • Geographic Jurisdictional Data
  • Preliminary Liability Indicators

3. How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise your right to opt-out, please use the protocol below or contact our Data Integrity Team. Once we receive and verify your request, we will cease sharing your personal information with third-party providers within the statutory timeframe (15 business days).

4. Non-Discrimination Statement

We will not discriminate against you for exercising your CCPA rights. Exercising your right to opt-out will not result in a denial of access to the Nodal Statutory Audit Engine, although it may impact our ability to match you with a qualified legal professional.
