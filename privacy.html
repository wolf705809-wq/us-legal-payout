1. Intent and Scope of Data Processing

This Protocol governs the collection, synchronization, and utilization of data processed through the Nodal Statutory Audit Engine. Unlike traditional lead generation, Nodal utilizes 256-bit statutory alignment to mitigate information asymmetry between insurance algorithms and incident victims. We collect data to provide objective case valuations based on federal and state liability frameworks.

2. Categories of Information Collected

We collect and process the following categories of Personal Information (PI) under CCPA/CPRA and GDPR-aligned standards:

  • Statutory Identifiers: Name, IP address, device fingerprinting, and unique session IDs generated by the Audit Engine.
  • Incident Metadata: Jurisdictional data (Zip Code), timestamp of occurrence, and classification of incident (e.g., Commercial Trucking vs. Passenger Vehicle).
  • Liability Indicators: Self-reported fault assessments, emergency response status (Ambulance/ER), and preliminary damage estimates.
  • Contact Synchronization: Telephone numbers and email addresses intended for statutory report delivery.

3. TCPA Consent & Automated Communications

By interacting with the Nodal Audit Engine and providing your telephone number, you provide your express written consent to be contacted via telephone, SMS, or MMS. This includes communications via automated dialing systems (ADSP) and pre-recorded messages. This consent applies even if your number is listed on a state or federal "Do Not Call" registry.

4. Data Sovereignty and CCPA Rights

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), California residents maintain specific rights regarding their "Statutory Footprint":

  • Right to Know: You may request a disclosure of the categories of data synced by Nodal.
  • Right to Opt-Out: You may exercise your "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" right via our dedicated portal.
  • Right to Correction: You may request adjustment of inaccurate incident metadata.

5. Strategic Data Sharing (Lead Matching)

To fulfill the "Recovery Potential" identified by our engine, Nodal may share validated incident data with a curated network of Third-Party Legal Service Providers. This sharing is conducted via secure API hooks to ensure data integrity. These providers are strictly prohibited from using your data for purposes outside the scope of your specific legal inquiry.
